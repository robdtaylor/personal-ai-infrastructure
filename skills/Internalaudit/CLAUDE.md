## Audit Programme Management

### Programme vs Plan vs Schedule

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| Programme | Overall approach to auditing | 3-year cycle, risk-based, all processes |
| Plan | Specific audit details | Scope, criteria, team, timing |
| Schedule | Calendar of audits | Q1: Machining, Q2: Assembly, etc. |

### Risk-Based Scheduling

**High Priority (Audit more frequently):**
- Processes with high customer impact
- Areas with recent nonconformances
- New or changed processes
- Customer complaint related areas
- Previous audit findings open
- High-risk PFMEA items

**Standard Priority:**
- Stable processes
- Good historical performance
- No recent changes
- No open corrective actions

### Coverage Matrix

Track audit coverage over the cycle:

| Process/Clause | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|----------------|--------|--------|--------|
| 4.1-4.4 Context | Q1 | - | Q1 |
| 5.1-5.3 Leadership | Q2 | - | Q2 |
| 6.1-6.3 Planning | - | Q1 | - |
| 7.1-7.5 Support | Q3 | Q2 | Q3 |
| 8.1-8.7 Operation | Q1-Q4 | Q1-Q4 | Q1-Q4 |
| 9.1-9.3 Performance | Q4 | Q3 | Q4 |
| 10.1-10.3 Improvement | Q4 | Q4 | Q4 |
| Machining | Q1, Q3 | Q2, Q4 | Q1, Q3 |
| Assembly | Q2, Q4 | Q1, Q3 | Q2, Q4 |
| Final Test | Q2, Q4 | Q1, Q3 | Q2, Q4 |

## Audit Preparation Deep Dive

### Before the Audit

**2-4 Weeks Prior:**
1. Review scope and criteria
2. Assign audit team
3. Notify auditee
4. Schedule opening/closing meetings
5. Arrange logistics (room, access)

**1 Week Prior:**
1. Review previous audit results
2. Review relevant procedures
3. Check open corrective actions
4. Review recent quality data (scrap, complaints)
5. Prepare checklist
6. Review FMEA/Control Plans for area

**Day Before:**
1. Confirm schedule with auditee
2. Print/prepare documents
3. Review checklist
4. Confirm team members
5. Prepare audit kit

### Audit Kit

- Checklist/audit forms
- Previous audit reports
- Relevant procedures
- Notebook and pen
- Calculator
- Measuring equipment (if product audit)
- Camera (if permitted)
- Safety equipment (if production floor)

## Interview Techniques

### Opening Questions

Start broad, then narrow:
- "Can you explain your role in this process?"
- "Walk me through how this operation works"
- "What are the key quality requirements?"

### Probing Questions

Dig deeper into responses:
- "How do you know that?"
- "Can you show me evidence?"
- "What happens if X occurs?"
- "How was that verified?"

### Verification Questions

Confirm understanding:
- "So if I understand correctly..."
- "Let me summarize what you've said..."
- "Is this the document you're referring to?"

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Uncooperative auditee:**
- Stay professional and calm
- Explain audit purpose
- Escalate if necessary

**Overly helpful auditee:**
- Keep focused on scope
- Verify claims with evidence
- Don't accept verbal assurances alone

**Defensive auditee:**
- Acknowledge their perspective
- Focus on requirements
- Use evidence, not opinion

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen actively
- Take detailed notes
- Follow up on unclear answers
- Thank participants

**Don't:**
- Be confrontational
- Jump to conclusions
- Accept "we always do that" without evidence
- Audit outside scope
- Promise outcomes

## Evidence Collection and Documentation

### Types of Evidence

| Type | Description | Weight |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Documentary | Written records, procedures | High |
| Physical | Observation of conditions, products | High |
| Testimonial | Verbal statements from personnel | Medium |
| Analytical | Data analysis, trends | High |

### Evidence Quality

**Good evidence is:**
- Objective (fact-based)
- Verifiable (can be confirmed)
- Relevant (relates to requirement)
- Sufficient (enough to conclude)

**Weak evidence:**
- Hearsay
- Opinion
- Single isolated instance
- Unverifiable claim

### Note-Taking Best Practices

```
Observation: [What you saw/heard]
Document ID: [Reference number, revision]
Date/Time: [When observed]
Location: [Where]
Person: [Who provided information]
Requirement: [What standard requires]
Evaluation: [Conformance/Nonconformance/Unable to determine]
```

| 10-50 items | 10 items | Representative |

## Writing Effective Findings

### Finding Structure

Good findings include:
1. **Requirement**: What the standard/procedure requires
2. **Evidence**: What was observed (specific, objective)
3. **Conclusion**: How evidence shows nonconformance

### Examples

**Weak Finding:**
"Training records are incomplete"

**Strong Finding:**
"IATF 16949, clause 7.2, requires demonstration of competence. Review of training records for CNC operators in the Machine Shop revealed that 3 of 10 operators (names: X, Y, Z) had no documented training on the current revision (Rev C) of Work Instruction WI-MS-023 'CNC Lathe Operation'. The work instruction was revised on 2024-06-15, and no retraining was documented."

### Finding Categories

**Systemic Nonconformance:**
- Affects entire system
- Pattern across multiple instances
- Usually Major

**Process Nonconformance:**
- Specific to one process
- May be Major or Minor depending on impact

**Isolated Nonconformance:**
- Single instance
- Usually Minor

**Documentation Nonconformance:**
- Missing or inadequate documented information
- May be Major or Minor depending on impact

## Corrective Action Verification

### Verification Methods

| Method | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| Document review | Procedure changes, records |
| Re-audit | Process or systemic issues |
| Data review | Effectiveness of actions |
| Interview | Competence, awareness |

### Verification Checklist

- [ ] Root cause addresses actual cause, not symptom
- [ ] Corrective action targets root cause
- [ ] Action is implemented (not just planned)
- [ ] Evidence of implementation exists
- [ ] Effectiveness can be measured
- [ ] Recurrence has been prevented
- [ ] Similar areas have been checked

### Common Verification Issues

**Problem:** Action addresses symptom, not cause
**Solution:** Challenge root cause analysis, ask "why" again

**Problem:** Action is "retraining" only
**Solution:** Ask what prevents reoccurrence if operator leaves

**Problem:** No objective evidence of effectiveness
**Solution:** Define measurable success criteria

## LPA Implementation Guide

### Building an LPA Programme

**Step 1: Define Scope**
- Which processes/areas to include
- Which characteristics to audit
- Integration with existing audits

**Step 2: Create Checklist**
- Focus on standardized work
- High-risk items from PFMEA
- Visual management elements
- Safety items
- Customer-specific requirements

**Step 3: Define Structure**
- Audit layers (Team Leader, Supervisor, Manager, etc.)
- Frequency at each layer
- Escalation process for findings

**Step 4: Train Auditors**
- Checklist explanation
- Finding documentation
- Immediate correction authority

**Step 5: Implement**
- Start with pilot area
- Refine checklist based on feedback
- Expand to all areas

**Step 6: Monitor and Improve**
- Track completion rates
- Analyze finding trends
- Continuous improvement

### Sample LPA Checklist Items

**Safety:**
- [ ] PPE being worn correctly
- [ ] Safety guards in place
- [ ] Aisles clear

**5S:**
- [ ] Work area organized
- [ ] Tools in designated location
- [ ] No unnecessary items

**Standardized Work:**
- [ ] Work instruction at station
- [ ] Operator following sequence
- [ ] Cycle time within standard

**Quality:**
- [ ] Gages calibrated (check sticker)
- [ ] First piece approved
- [ ] SPC charts current
- [ ] Defective parts identified/segregated

**Documentation:**
- [ ] Traveler/routing complete
- [ ] Inspection records current
- [ ] Lot traceability maintained

### LPA Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Audit completion rate | >95% | Weekly |
| Finding closure rate | <5 days avg | Weekly |
| Repeat findings | <10% | Monthly |
| Finding trend | Decreasing | Monthly |

## Preparing for Certification Audits

### 6 Months Before

- [ ] Review all procedures for IATF alignment
- [ ] Complete internal audit cycle
- [ ] Verify all corrective actions closed
- [ ] Review customer complaints and actions
- [ ] Verify all required documents exist
- [ ] Plan management review

### 3 Months Before

- [ ] Complete management review
- [ ] Verify training records current
- [ ] Verify calibration current
- [ ] Review supplier performance data
- [ ] Update quality objectives status
- [ ] Stage 1 readiness assessment

### 1 Month Before

- [ ] Final internal audit
- [ ] Close all open items
- [ ] Verify all records accessible
- [ ] Brief all personnel on audit
- [ ] Arrange logistics (rooms, schedule)
- [ ] Prepare document packages

### Common Certification Audit Findings

**Clause 4 - Context:**
- Interested parties not identified
- No risk assessment documented
- Process interactions unclear

**Clause 5 - Leadership:**
- Quality policy not communicated
- Responsibilities not documented
- Customer focus not evident

**Clause 6 - Planning:**
- FMEA incomplete or outdated
- Actions for risks not implemented
- Quality objectives not measurable

**Clause 7 - Support:**
- Training needs not identified
- Calibration overdue
- Documented information not controlled

**Clause 8 - Operation:**
- Control plans don't match process
- Work instructions outdated
- Special characteristics not controlled

**Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation:**
- Internal audits not covering all processes
- Management review incomplete
- Customer satisfaction not monitored

**Clause 10 - Improvement:**
- Corrective actions not effective
- No evidence of continual improvement
- Root cause analysis inadequate

### Audit Day Tips

1. Have key documents ready (policy, objectives, org chart)
2. Assign guides for each auditor
3. Keep schedule on track
4. Take notes during audit
5. Don't argue with findings
6. Ask for clarification if unclear
7. Provide requested evidence promptly
8. Be honest about weaknesses
