## Dual-Perspective Guidance (CRITICAL)

**Every tariff, customs, and trade compliance response MUST include both Conservative and Aggressive views.**

### Why Two Perspectives?

Trade compliance exists on a spectrum. The same facts can support multiple legitimate positions:
- **Conservative**: What a cautious compliance officer would do (minimise audit risk)
- **Aggressive**: What a strategic trade consultant would explore (minimise duty cost)

Neither is inherently right or wrong. The best approach depends on:
- Company risk tolerance
- Transaction value/volume
- Existing compliance track record
- Strategic importance of the trade route
- Available resources to defend positions

**Facts:** UK company imports German-made automotive sensors, adds UK wiring harness, exports to EU customer.

> Without detailed BOM analysis, assume the sensor does not undergo sufficient transformation in UK. Export to EU without EUR.1, customer pays MFN duty (typically 3-4.5%). Avoid risk of origin fraud penalties.
>
> *Rationale: UK processing may be insufficient for tariff shift. Better to let customer claim back if they can prove origin.*

> Analyse value-add in UK. If UK content (labour, wiring harness, packaging, testing) exceeds 50% ex-works price, goods may qualify as UK origin under TCA cumulation rules. Issue supplier declaration, customer claims zero duty.
>
> *Rationale: TCA allows EU content to count toward UK origin. Combined UK+German content likely meets threshold.*

## Expert Guidance Principles

When assisting with shipping, customs, and logistics queries:

1. **Always provide both perspectives** - Conservative for risk mitigation, Aggressive for cost optimisation.
2. **Always verify current rates** - Tariff rates change frequently. Direct users to official sources for final confirmation.
3. **Consider total landed cost** - Don't just look at duty rates; include freight, insurance, handling, VAT/GST.
4. **Check for trade agreements** - Many goods qualify for preferential rates under FTAs.
5. **Document requirements vary** - Different entry types have different documentation needs.
6. **Rules of origin are complex** - Don't assume preferential treatment; it must be proven.

## Detailed Country Guides

### United Kingdom

#### HMRC Import Procedures

**Standard Imports:**
1. Obtain EORI number (starts with GB)
2. Classify goods using UK Trade Tariff
3. Determine customs procedure code (CPC)
4. Submit import declaration via CDS
5. Pay duty and VAT (or defer)
6. Receive goods release

**Simplified Procedures:**
- CFSP (Customs Freight Simplified Procedures)
- Entry in Declarant's Records (EIDR)
- Simplified Declaration Procedure (SDP)

#### UK Customs Procedure Codes (CPCs)

Common automotive CPCs:
- **40 00 000**: Free circulation, simultaneous payment
- **42 00 000**: Free circulation, VAT exempt (re-export)
- **51 00 000**: Inward Processing Relief
- **61 00 000**: Re-importation

#### UK Special Procedures

**Inward Processing (IP):**
- Import goods duty-free for processing and re-export
- Must apply for authorization from HMRC
- Common for automotive component assembly

**Customs Warehousing:**
- Store goods without paying duty
- Pay when goods released for free circulation
- Useful for buffer stock management

### United States

#### CBP Entry Types

| Type | Code | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| Consumption | 01 | Standard import for US market |
| Warehouse | 21 | Bonded warehouse storage |
| Foreign Trade Zone | 06 | FTZ admission |
| Temporary Import | 23 | ATA Carnet, temporary use |
| Drawback | 47 | Duty refund on re-export |

#### CBP Partner Government Agencies (PGAs)

Automotive imports may require clearance from:
- **EPA**: Emissions compliance (Form 3520-1)
- **DOT/NHTSA**: Safety compliance (HS-7 Declaration)
- **FCC**: Electronic equipment with radio transmitters

#### Section 232 Tariffs - Steel & Aluminum

**Covered Products:**
- All steel articles under Chapter 72 and 73
- Aluminum articles under Chapter 76
- Certain derivative articles (auto parts with steel/aluminum content)

**Exemptions:**
- CUSMA-qualifying goods (with proper documentation)
- Goods from countries with quota agreements
- Certain processed products (check exclusions list)

**Documentation:**
- Steel/aluminum content declaration
- Country of melt and pour for steel
- Country of smelt and cast for aluminum

#### Foreign Trade Zones (FTZs)

Benefits for automotive:
- Defer duty until goods leave zone
- Eliminate duty on re-exports
- Inverted tariff benefits (pay lower rate on finished goods)
- Weekly entry filing

### Canada

#### CBSA Tariff Treatments

| Code | Description | Eligibility |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| MFN | Most Favored Nation | All WTO members |
| GPT | General Preferential Tariff | Developing countries |
| UST | United States Tariff | CUSMA-qualifying US goods |
| MXT | Mexico Tariff | CUSMA-qualifying Mexican goods |
| CPTPT | CPTPP Tariff | CPTPP member countries |

#### Canadian Import Process

1. **Pre-arrival**: Submit cargo data (ACI eManifest)
2. **Release**: Obtain release via RMD or EDI
3. **Accounting**: File B3 within 5 business days
4. **Payment**: Pay duties within 30 days (or use RPP)

#### CBSA Trade Programs

**Customs Self-Assessment (CSA):**
- Pre-approved importers and carriers
- Simplified border processing
- Post-importation accounting

**Partners in Protection (PIP):**
- Supply chain security program
- Equivalent to US C-TPAT
- Reduced examinations

## Automotive-Specific Guidance

### OEM Shipping Requirements

Most automotive OEMs require:
- ASN (Advance Ship Notice) via EDI 856
- Standardized packaging (AIAG/Odette guidelines)
- Bar-coded labels (AIAG B-10 format)
- Specific carrier routing

### Just-in-Time (JIT) Considerations

For JIT shipments:
- Pre-clearance essential to avoid production line stops
- Consider bonded carrier status
- Maintain buffer stock for customs delays
- Use simplified procedures where available

### IMDS/REACH Compliance

**IMDS (International Material Data System):**
- Required by most OEMs
- Material composition declaration
- Not directly customs-related but often requested

**REACH (EU/UK):**
- Chemical registration requirements
- Only Representative requirement for non-EU importers
- Affects UK imports from non-UK sources

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Goods Held at Customs

**Possible Causes:**
1. Missing or incorrect documentation
2. Classification discrepancy
3. Valuation query
4. Compliance hold (PGA)
5. Random examination

**Resolution Steps:**
1. Contact customs broker immediately
2. Provide requested documentation
3. Consider filing protest if classification disputed
4. Pay under protest to release goods

### Duty Refund Situations

**When to claim:**
- Overpayment of duty
- Goods rejected and returned
- Duty drawback on re-export
- Retrospective FTA claim

**Time Limits:**
- UK: 3 years from import date
- US: 180 days for protest, 5 years for drawback
- Canada: 4 years for refund claims

### Valuation Disputes

**Related Party Transactions:**
- Must demonstrate arm's length pricing
- Use comparable uncontrolled price method
- Document transfer pricing policy

**Assists:**
- Tools, dies, molds provided to supplier
- Must be added to customs value
- Apportion over expected production quantity
